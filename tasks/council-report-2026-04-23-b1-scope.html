LLM Council — B1 Preregistration Scope

2026-04-23 UTC · Fired after Probe 4 verdict SESSION_CONFOUND (c419391) · 5 advisors, 5 peer reviews, chairman synthesis
Question
Scope the B1 preregistration for the session-structure sweep authorized by the Probe 4 SESSION_CONFOUND verdict. Probe 4 showed combos 1298 and 664 both ~80% trade-concentrated in SES_2 (ex-RTH) on the 1h held-out test partition, with SES_1 RTH failing absolute for both. What is the right scope for B1 — a session-structure sweep across multiple combos on 1h NQ — subject to Rule 1 (stage naming & multiplicity denominator) and Rule 2 (P(PASS|H1) and P(PASS|H0) per gate)? Four candidate scopes presented: A top-k confirmation on test, B train-select / test-confirm, C portfolio-level test, D sub-session partitioning on {865, 1298, 664}.

Chairman Verdict

Do not launch B1 in any of the four proposed scopes. Replace with a two-track sequence: Scope D as a 10-minute mechanism check, then a paper-trade preregistration on the combo(s) that survive.
The combined weight of constraint #5 (B1 passing doesn't authorize deployment), 3× partition exhaustion (Probes 2, 3, 4), and two independent reviewer ceilings — wall-clock power bound (Reviewer 2) and shared-parameter-geometry making effective n possibly = 1 (Reviewer 3) — means B1 under any scope is narrative characterization whose information value is dominated by forward paper-trade data on the carve-outs already in hand (Reviewer 1: the clock generates the only genuinely fresh partition). Probe 4's 3.45× overnight ratio cannot be used to power-calibrate any gate run on the same 1h test partition (circular — all 4 reviewers caught this) but can serve as an a priori sizing input for forward paper-trade, because forward is a different partition.
The one thing to do first Run Scope D as a 10-minute patch to tasks/_probe4_readout.py — split SES_2 into 16:00–18:00 (post-RTH + settlement halt) and 18:00–09:30 (GLOBEX overnight) on combos {865, 1298, 664} against the 1h test partition, and read out per-trade net $ and n_trades per cell. The answer to "is this overnight edge or settlement-halt artifact?" decides whether the paper-trade preregistration goes forward at all.

Advisor Responses (full text)

The Contrarian· "Do not launch B1 until partition exhaustion is priced"

B1 Preregistration Attack. Rule 1 stage-naming sleight-of-hand is already embedded in every scope. Scopes A, C, and D all propose running on the 1h test partition. That partition has been consumed three times. Call it what it is: the "test partition" is now a training-adjacent surface. Pricing this as a "Bonferroni-1" single test is the exact 2026-04-21 Probe 3 error dressed in new clothes. The honest denominator is researcher-DOF-inflated, bounded below by 3 prior probes + whatever selection pressure produced the combo set.

Fatal flaws per scope

Scope A — selection on noise (family Probe 1 falsified at N_1.3=4 vs gate 10). Top-k-by-training-Sharpe implicitly conditions on session structure. P(PASS|H0) is inflated by the training-gross-Sharpe floor.

Scope B — the "cleanest" and therefore most dangerous. Selection criterion is session-based and the clock doesn't know about the train/test split. Session structure leaks across the split. You are not Stage-1/Stage-2 separating anything — you're selecting on session, confirming on session, and calling it partition discipline.

Scope C — "multiplicity denominator = 1"? No. Portfolio construction (equal-weight vs Sharpe-weight vs correlation-pruned) is three gardens-of-forking-paths.

Scope D — the 17:00–18:00 CME settlement halt means Probe 4's SES_2 includes a window where no trades should execute anyway. Sub-partitioning might just recover the Probe-3 narrow-wraparound result with extra steps. The "16:00–18:00 doesn't contribute" kill criterion is nearly pre-determined by microstructure.

Rule 2 attack

Every scope proposes Welch-t or Sharpe-difference gates. P(PASS|H1) requires an assumed effect size. Probe 3 and Probe 4 both observed ~3× overnight/RTH ratios — but those are the training data for H1. Using observed ratios to power-calibrate a confirmation test is circular.

Real kill criterion

Pre-register that SES_1 RTH must remain negative or zero on an independent NQ-1h data vendor's partition (Polygon, Databento). If RTH is net-profitable on clean vendor data, the session-structure thesis dies. None of A/B/C/D proposes this.

Recommendation: do not launch B1 until partition exhaustion is priced.

The First Principles Thinker· "You're three probes deep into a falsified family — deploy or stop"

Stop. Before answering A/B/C/D, ask what B1 actually buys you. Three candidate questions are getting conflated:

Q1. Does 1h NQ Z-score mean-reversion have a real edge? Probe 1 already answered this at the family level: no. N_1.3 = 4 of 1500. Sunset fired. Closed.

Q2. Is there a tradable pocket inside the falsified family? Probes 2-4 answered: yes, for {865, 1298, 664}, concentrated overnight. You have three carve-outs + mechanism narrative (3.45× ratio, ~80% trade concentration, SES_1 fails absolute).

Q3. Does the overnight-structural story generalize to a basin large enough to justify deployment confidence? This is what B1 ostensibly tests — but constraint #5 says B1 passing doesn't authorize deployment. So B1 is research-stage family characterization that by the user's own hard constraints cannot close the deployment loop. It produces a narrative asset, not a decision input.

Rule 1 honestly

Test partition consumed 3× on this exact signal family. Researcher DOF across Probes 2, 3, 4 has already biased which patterns you're chasing. Scope B is the only one that re-imposes partition discipline, but it pays full Stage 1 multiplicity on a sweep you already know the family answer to.

Rule 2 honestly

Under H0 "no overnight structural edge," 3 combos already refute H0. Under H1 "overnight edge generalizes," effective n ≈ 3 post-Probes 2-4. The likelihood ratio from another 20-50 combo basin is bounded — you're mostly re-measuring what you already measured.

Reframe

The productive question isn't "does the basin confirm session-structure." It's: what is the minimum-ceremony preregistration that authorizes a paper-trade decision on the combos you already have? Specify (i) overnight sub-window binding (Scope D's split picks your execution clock), (ii) forward-walking paper-trade protocol with stopping rules, (iii) ex-ante sizing floor tied to per-trade net $, not Sharpe. That answers a decision, not a narrative.

Recommendation: don't run B1 as scoped. Run Scope D as a characterization footnote, then write the paper-trade preregistration.

The Outsider· "Is SES_2 concentration an edge or a sampling artifact?"

Wait. Before engaging with this document, things it treats as settled that a fresh reader cannot parse:

(1) What is "session-structure inefficiency" claiming to be? The document glides from "80% of trades are in SES_2" directly to "the edge is overnight-structural." These are not the same claim. NQ overnight has thinner volume → mechanically more Z-score crossings. The signal will fire more often overnight without there being more edge per opportunity. The per-trade $ numbers (+$1,844 vs -$194 for 1298) do suggest more than just count — but has anyone shown the holding-time / opportunity-cost axis? Overnight stretches take longer to revert, tying up the book. I don't see it anywhere.

(2) Rule 1. Scopes A, C, D are the fourth dip on the test partition. Only B has clean hygiene. Denominator = 1 under a single pre-registered contrast on B; A/C/D need a partition-reuse discount nobody has rigorously computed (Probe 3 council failed at this).

(3) Rule 2. I genuinely cannot estimate P(PASS|H1) vs P(PASS|H0) for the proposed gates — and nobody has defined H1 precisely. "Overnight Z-score MR has economic edge" vs "session-ratio pattern generalizes" require different simulations. The document assumes we know.

(4) SES_2 spans 16:00–18:00 ET including the settlement halt. Liquidity collapses AND formal trading halt. Bundling with GLOBEX overnight is a category error. Only Scope D addresses this.

Scoring

B > D > A > C. B has cleanest stage discipline but compound selection → confirmation is what Probes 2/3/4 keep failing. D is small-N but surgical. A reuses test without pricing it. C portfolio-averages away the exact heterogeneity we're trying to characterize.

Kill criterion wanted

If pure-overnight 18:00–09:30 alone doesn't dominate, the "session-structure" framing is wrong and the real finding is "thin-liquidity Z-score noise harvesting" — different risk profile, not an edge anyone should size on.

Recommendation: Run D first as a cheap mechanism-check. Only if D shows 18:00–09:30 dominance, run B with that as prespecified screening. Do not run A or C.

Strongest picks & biggest blind spots (5 anonymized reviewers)

Strongest: First Principles picked 3×, Contrarian picked 2×. No reviewer endorsed Expansionist, Outsider, or Executor as strongest.

Biggest blind spot — 5/5 unanimous: the Expansionist. Every reviewer independently flagged the same failures: (1) the Bayes factor BF ≈ 145× uses Probe 3/4's 3.45× ratio as the H1 effect size — but that ratio was estimated from the same partition the gate would run on, so the BF is circular false precision; (2) claiming "denominator = 1 single prespecified family-level test" while enumerating a 5-6 axis tensor decomposition is exactly the garden-of-forking-paths Rule 1 exists to prevent; (3) chronological discovery/confirmation split on an already-3×-consumed test partition doesn't restore partition discipline — it subdivides an exhausted resource.

Blind spots the council caught (what ALL 5 advisors missed — one per reviewer):
  • Reviewer 1: Opportunity cost of B1 compute vs forward paper-trade runway. Forward data is the only genuinely fresh partition — generated by the clock, not by researchers. Every week on B1 is a week of uncollected paper-trade data on a half-made decision.
  • Reviewer 2: Wall-clock sample-size ceiling. 1h test partition is ~1.5 years; after restricting to overnight-only and splitting session × sub-window × combo, per-cell trade counts fall below the n ≥ 50 floor Probes 2-4 established as load-bearing. B1's power is physics-bounded, not method-bounded.
  • Reviewer 3: {865, 1298, 664} may share common entry-parameter structure (stop-distance × z-threshold × EMA window jointly favoring overnight fills). Effective n may be 1, not 3, collapsing every power calculation. Also: $5 RT friction is session-invariant by assumption, but overnight bid-ask widens — the cost model itself may fail in SES_2.
  • Reviewer 4: Probe 4's SESSION_CONFOUND may be the ceiling, not a springboard. The carve-outs and the family-level Probe 1 falsification are plausibly the same phenomenon (thin-liquidity noise harvesting). B1 risks characterizing an artifact.
  • Reviewer 5: No advisor computed VOI or specified a capital threshold. The user's $50k account may not need a 3-combo basin — single-combo paper-trade on 865 may be the economically rational next step.

Full Chairman Synthesis

Where the Council Agrees

Scope E (Expansionist) is the most dangerous submission despite looking the most operational — all 5 reviewers independently flagged it. Claiming "denominator = 1" while enumerating a 5-6 axis tensor decomposition is the Rule 1 garden-of-forking-paths violation that sank Probe 3's original council. The BF ≈ 145× is computed against an H1 effect size lifted from the same partition the gate is about to run on.

Scope C is dead. Executor, Outsider, Contrarian, and First Principles all rank it lowest. Portfolio aggregation conflates session-decomposition with covariance structure without adding inferential signal.

The 1h test partition is running out of inferential room. Contrarian first-class, First Principles routes around it, Outsider calls A/C/D "fourth dip," Reviewers 1-4 all endorse.

Amendment 1's SES_2 is a category error — bundles 16:00-18:00 settlement halt with GLOBEX overnight. Any binding that keeps SES_2 monolithic is wrong.

Where the Council Clashes

Launch vs don't launch. First Principles (backed by Reviewers 1, 2, 5) says B1 under any scope is re-characterization of a falsified family that cannot close the deployment loop — route to paper-trade prereg instead. Contrarian (backed by Reviewers 3, 4) says even paper-trade is downstream; independent-vendor replication (Polygon/Databento 1h) is the only move that breaks researcher-DOF. Expansionist and Executor take the framed question at face value. This is the real fork.

Mechanism vs basin. Outsider and Executor both endorse D first, for different reasons: Outsider wants kill criterion (pure overnight or settlement-halt artifact?); Executor wants cheap 10-minute screen before committing to the 4-5h Scope B. First Principles accepts D as "footnote" but not a gate.

Effective n. Reviewer 3's catch — {865, 1298, 664} may be one parameter realization, not three — directly undercuts Expansionist's 30-combo basin assumption. If effective n = 1, every power calculation in every prereg is overstated.

The Recommendation (full)

Do not launch B1 in any of the four proposed scopes. Replace with a two-track sequence, in order:

1. Scope D as a 10-minute mechanistic check only. Executor's specs: modify tasks/_probe4_readout.py to add a 3rd session bucket splitting SES_2 into 16:00–18:00 and 18:00–09:30 ET on {865, 1298, 664}, 1h test partition. Call it what it is — a mechanism footnote, not a family-level confirmation. Stage = characterization. Denominator = 3 (combos) × 2 (sub-windows).

2. Paper-trade preregistration on the combo(s) that survive D. Forward-walking protocol with ex-ante stopping rules, per-trade net-$ sizing floor (not Sharpe — Reviewer 5's economic anchor), overnight sub-window bound by D's output, clock-generated data (the only genuinely unconsumed partition available).

On Probe 4's 3.45× ratio: cannot be used to power-calibrate any gate on the same 1h test partition (circular — 4/5 reviewers caught). Can be used as a priori effect-size for paper-trade sizing because forward data is a different partition.

On Contrarian's independent-vendor replication: note as future option if paper-trade equivocates, but paper-trade forward data addresses partition-reuse at zero marginal data-acquisition cost.
