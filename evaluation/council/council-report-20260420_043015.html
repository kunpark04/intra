Chairman's Verdict

Where the Council Agrees

Four of five advisors converge on a single diagnosis: the shipped pipeline has not been shown to produce alpha distinguishable from "50-combo concentration of a pre-filtered pool." The claim "ranker is noise but truncation is load-bearing" is logically unstable because the full-pool blow-up test compares top-50 to the full 500-combo pool — not to random-50 from the same pool. The decisive missing experiment is a random-K null distribution, not a rolling retrain and not a new ranker. Three of five also called out that V4 was trained on the same partition that generated the combos — a second selection effect stacked on the first.

Where the Council Clashes

The split is audit vs. exploit. The Expansionist argues the concentration insight is itself the franchise: scaling (new instruments, diversity selectors, more sizing channels) is the right move; auditing a single basket optimizes the wrong variable. Everyone else argues the mechanism is not yet established; scaling an unverified edge is the textbook way to turn a selection artifact into a large loss. The Executor occupies a third position — rolling-retrain Jaccard — which two reviewers correctly objected measures the stability of something already known to be noise. The Expansionist's position is coherent conditional on the mechanism being real; the majority position is that this condition has not been met.

Blind Spots the Council Caught (peer review only)

  1. V4 is the mechanism, not a side-leak. If V4 memorized which combos survive in-partition, any random-50 drawn after V4 inherits that leak. A naive null test would falsely confirm concentration as the edge. The null must run pre-V4 or use a V4 refit on a clean partition.
  2. The post-gate pool is itself a selection artifact. MIN_TRADES_GATE=500 and v11 sweep ranges were human-tuned. "Positive expectancy of the post-gate pool" is not a free prior — it's a curated one.
  3. 641 trades ≈ one macro regime. The 2020–2025 post-gate pool is a survivor of a specific rate/vol environment. Even a diversity-optimal selector over a regime-conditioned pool ships regime risk mislabeled as alpha.
  4. Capacity-correlation frontier is untested. No one measured whether the 13,814 pool supports 2, 4, or 8 decorrelated 50-baskets, or whether aggregate Sharpe compounds linearly. This is where the Expansionist's thesis is confirmable or killable.

The Recommendation

Run the random-50 null test before anything else, but structure it so it can't be fooled by V4 leakage or post-gate tuning. Two arms, both required: (a) random-50 vs. top-50 vs. bottom-50 drawn from the post-gate pool, routed through a V4 refit on a held-out partition; (b) the same three-way comparison on the pre-gate pool. The Executor's rolling-retrain Jaccard is deferred — it measures noise stability of a ranker whose value-add is unestablished. The Expansionist's scaling program is deferred — the mechanism has to be real before building a factory on it. If shipped-50 sits below the 75th percentile of the random-50 distribution with a leakage-corrected V4, the 2.13 Sharpe is a reconstruction, not a forecast.

The Contrarian · Fatal-flaw audit · selection bias

Fatal flaw in plain sight: you cannot simultaneously claim "the ranker is noise" and "top-50 is load-bearing for regime diversity." If the ranker is indistinguishable from random, then "top-50 by raw Sharpe" is just "top-50 by in-sample Sharpe on the training partition" — the most classic overfitting selector in quant finance. The 2.13 Sharpe is a reconstruction error, not a forecast.

Jaccard 0.653 doesn't save you. Ordinal-shift perturbs input ordering, not retraining on held-out data. Combos stable across ±180d shifts are stable because the underlying trades don't move much — not because the selection procedure generalizes.

The full-pool ablation is being misread. "Dropping top-K blows up to 43% ruin" does not prove top-K is principled — it proves the 500-combo uniform sample contains trash. A random 50-combo subsample would likely also outperform the full pool; any concentration reduces variance. You haven't compared top-50 vs. random-50 vs. bottom-50. Until you do, "concentration is architectural" is just "small portfolios have lower variance than large ones."

V4 trained on the same partition = a second leak stacked on the first.

Next: random-50 vs. top-50 vs. bottom-50 through V4, evaluated on a true walk-forward 2024+ partition. Don't trade real money until you run that.

The First Principles Thinker · Reframe · null hypothesis

You're asking the wrong questions. Rebuild from zero: 13,814 combos, keep 50, ranker is provably random. So: you're running a 50-combo portfolio sampled by a process statistically equivalent to uniform random selection from a pre-filtered pool. Everything else is narrative.

Q1 reframed: The right counterfactual isn't full-pool-no-topK. It's: draw 1,000 independent random 50-combo baskets from the 13,814 post-gate pool, run s6_net on each, plot the Sharpe distribution. If your shipped 50 sits at the 50th percentile, your ranker contributed zero and your Sharpe is just what a 50-basket of V4-filtered combos looks like.

Q2 reframed: The posture is coherent but described wrong. It's not "ranker noise + truncation signal." It's "post-gate pool has positive expectancy; concentration to ~50 controls correlation; which 50 doesn't matter." Ship that story. It's defensible. The current story isn't.

The Expansionist · Concentration = alpha · scale the operator

The concentration insight is the alpha, and the council is about to underprice it. Ranker signal is noise, yet the pipeline ships a 2.13 Sharpe with 0.02% ruin. The value isn't in picking combos — it's in the portfolio-construction operator that compresses any reasonable pool into 50 regime-diverse bets whose losing streaks decorrelate. You've stumbled onto a near-model-free edge. The "ranker" is a random-basis sampler; the architecture is the product.

If it generalizes:

  1. Instrument multiplication. Same operator on MES, MGC, MCL, MBT = a portfolio factory. 5 instruments × uncorrelated 2.13 Sharpe ≈ aggregate 4+.
  2. Ranker arbitrage. Replace v12 with a diversity-maximizing selector (DPP, k-medoids, max-entropy). Cheap to try.
  3. Capacity. 50 combos = 50 independent sizing channels. Shipped 5% risk is probably 3–5× under-levered.

Next: swap the ranker for a diversity objective on the same pool and compare s6_net. Validate the mechanism, because the mechanism is the franchise.
